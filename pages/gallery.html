follow the journey of our wine grapes

These are beautiful, ripe red wine grapes. They are plump and round, and their deep red is striking against the green leaves of the vine they are growing on.


The grapes are tightly packed together a bunch. The texture of the grapes appears to be smooth and firm.

They are bunched in neat clusters hanging from their vines, and their gleaming red skin in the morning Italian sun provides a beautiful contrast. They look delicious and ready to be picked.

These grapes are waiting to be picked and used by our happy customers for wine production. Every year we supply the U.K. wine-community to make a delicious, complex and elegant drink.

The Mediterranean climate of Italy, with warm summers and mild winters, is perfectly ideal for grape growing. And these grapes are mouthwatering.

The Italian vineyard is a picturesque and tranquil place, surrounded by lush green hills, and with rows of grapevines stretching out as far as the eye can see. The grapes - grown traditionally - are tended to by skilled and dedicated vintners.

The vineyards are characterized by their beautifully manicured landscape, well-maintained rows, and traditional farming methods passed down through generations. The grapes are hand-picked at the perfect stage of ripeness.

The quality of the grapes determines the quality of the wine, which is why winemakers place a great emphasis on selecting the perfect grapes for the job. We strive for all our customers to be as ecstatic about their wine grapes as we are.

We offer a variety of red wine grapes, including our most popular, Montepulciano. Read more about it here.

Our most popular white grape. Trebbiano wine is usually greenish-yellow on the eye, full-bodied and with a strong balance of tropical fruit, spice, and minerality. Read more about it here.

The white wine made from these Trebbiano grapes is usually light and easy to drink. With a moderate alcohol content, and a distinctive fruity and floral aroma. These wines are perfect for pairing with seafood, salads, and other light dishes.

We also offer Moscato. Famous for its sweet flavours of peaches and orange blossoms, Moscato is made up of perfumed aromas of ripe pear, mandarin orange, sweet Meyer lemon, honeysuckle, and orange blossom.Read more about it here.

Focused and hardworking - a team of vinters work together in the Italian vineyard to gather the wine grapes. The grapes are a deep red, indicating they are ready for harvest.

The wine grapes are loaded onto a lorry for delivery to London, with great care and attention to ensure that the grapes arrive in excellent condition.


The grapes are delivered in wooden boxes weighing approximately 8-9kg. They are carefully loaded onto the back of the lorry by a team of workers.

The wooden cases protect the grapes during transport, with ventilation openings to keep them fresh and cool. The lorry is also refrigerated to keep the grapes at the optimal temperature throughout the journey.


Extra care is taken so that the grapes arrive in London in a way that prevents any damage during transport.

After transport, the wine grapes are delivered and are in excellent condition, ready for crushing. We carefully inspect the grapes to confirm they are ready for wine-making.

The grapes have maintained their deep red colour, indicating that they are ripe and at the perfect stage for wine-making.

Every year we serve thousands of customers passionate about wine-making like we are. These grapes are ready to be collected or deliverd to the wine-making community in the U.K.

In addition to wine grapes, we also serve eating grapes grown in the same vineyard for our customers to enjoy. These grapes are plump, ripe and bursting with sweetness. They are usually larger than wine grapes and have glossy skin.

These are perfect for friends and family. They can be eaten fresh, as a healthy snack, or used in fruit salads, pastries, jams and jellies. And if you're feeling adventurous, try making your wine with them!
