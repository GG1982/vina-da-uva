We've carefully handpicked a selection of six flavourous wine grapes sourced from Italy for your enjoyment.

All of our grapes undergo a process of inspection to ensure that they meet the required quality standards

montepulcianowine grape

Montepulciano wines are famous for their soft flavours and deep colour pigments. Delicate tannins and a low acidity create a wine typically best consumed soon after bottling.

Sangiovesewine grape

Sangiovese wine is known for its high acidity, medium-plus tannins and savoury characters. Sniff a glass next time you'll find that aromas move toward dried cherries, figs and roses.

Shirazwine grape

Syrah or shiraz can produce deliciously dark wine. Its high tannins mean Shiraz wines are capable of ageing and improving over the decades. Expect a rich, deep flavour of dark fruits and a hint of coffee and spice.

merlotwine grape

Merlot's soft texture helps to give it a deliciously plummy, almost fruitcake-like flavour. It can be a little grassy and develops blackcurrant, blackberry, blueberry, chocolate and spice-like characters when fully ripe.

Trebbiano d'Abbruzzowine grape

Trebbiano d'Abruzzo is a white wine full of colour, sweet scents, duration of flavour and capability to age. In the right hands, the Trebbiano grapes result in some of the finest white wines available.

Moscatowine grape

The wine made from Moscato has an irresistible sweetness and refreshing fruit flavors! Most Moscato wines have high residual sugar levels, an opulent floral aroma with hints of honeysuckle, and flavor notes of white peach, apricot, and tropical fruit.
